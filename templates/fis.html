How They Are Priced

Fiscal policy is a critical component of a country's economic management. It involves the government's decisions regarding taxation, spending, and borrowing to influence the economy's overall health and stability. Fiscal policy is typically used to achieve specific economic objectives and can be expansionary or contractionary in nature.

How fiscal policy works?

In an expansionary fiscal policy, the government increases its spending and reduces taxes to stimulate economic growth. This approach is often employed during economic downturns or recessions to boost consumer and business spending, create jobs, and revitalize the economy. Lower taxes and increased public investment can lead to increased consumer confidence and higher demand for goods and services.
